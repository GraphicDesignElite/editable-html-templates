Important Notice for Non Profit Corporations

The mere filing or registration of the non-profit corporation papers (i.e., the Articles of Incorporation filed with the Secretary of State) does not give the organization tax-exempt status. There are time-sensitive steps to be taken. Disclosures must be made to the Internal Revenue Service that detail, among other things:

  1. The names and relationship of the directors, employees and officers;
  2. The proposed fund-raising methods;
  3. The names of any substantial contributors;
  4. How the funds will be consumed or distributed; AND
  5. The amount and kind of any unrelated business income.

It is important to file the requisite IRS documents to obtain tax-exempt status. For further information, please feel free to review some of the following important and relevant publications published by the IRS:

  • Publication 557-Tax-Exempt Status for Your Organization
  • Publication 578-Tax Information for Private Foundations
  • Publication 598-Tax on Unrelated Business Income
  • Package 1023-Application for Recognition of Exemption Under Section 501 (c)(3)
  • Package 1024-Application for Recognition of Exemption Under Section 501 (9)
  • Form SS-4 Application for Employer Identification Number
  • Form 990-Annual Information Return for Exempt Organization
  • Form 990PF-Annual Information Return for Private Foundation

These forms can be obtained either in person from your local IRS office, or they can be ordered by mail by calling 1-800-289-1040. In addition, you may also download IRS forms at http://www.irs.gov/.

NOTE: Merely filing the required Federal IRS forms will not grant you tax-exempt status at the State level. Please contact the appropriate state agency to qualify for state tax exemption. In most states, the Secretary of State or the Department of Revenue may be able to assist you. Should you desire to do so, you can use our trusted partner, Center For Nonprofit Creation, who works with our customers to help them do so. Their phone number is (310) 557-0804 or you can fill out their online form at http://www.123ezcorp.com/mycorp/index.asp.

For a non-profit corporation to qualify for 501(c)(3) Federal tax-exempt status, a timely filing of IRS form 1023 must be made. If you file on time, the tax-exemption is effective retroactively to the date on which your articles of incorporation were filed.

When the IRS approves the tax-exemption, then and ONLY then, can the non-profit corporation be assured that contributions are tax deductible, and that the corporation is a tax-exempt company.

In addition, please note that not all non-profit corporations are tax-exempt. A non-profit corporation may qualify for tax-exempt status for all or some of its income, for either Federal or State tax purposes, or for both.
